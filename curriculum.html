P.A.V.E. Sessions

Weekly Session Schedule 2023

Our modules are completed in monthly session format. Each month, the chosen topic will be focused on and discussed over the 4 week time schedule. Weekly sessions are facilitated by a PAVE team member and a Presenter chosen from the community.

The session layout is as follows:

    Welcome/ Icebreaker activity (10 mins)

    Participants get their food and settle down into focus mode

  • Check-in (10 mins): The class will review the topic from the previous week. Engage with participants about how/ if they implemented any new actions with their family at home.
  • Discussion session (45 mins): PAVE team member will introduce the new topic. Presenter will conduct the session through open discussion interactions with the class.
  • Wrap Up (15 mins): Presenter will close the session with questions/evaluation from class about what was learned or any questions. Pave team member or Presenter will give class “food for thought” concerning what to expect or think about before next week’s session.

Quarter 1

Module 1: Communication   Derrick Mitchell (Presenter)   January 2023

In this module we will be learning how to set aside time to talk and listen to each other. We will discuss how to create a safe, quiet space to communicate including turning off distractions (t.v., phones, computers, etc), being open minded about feelings (anger, sadness, joy, frustration, fear, anxiety, etc.) and how to begin when dealing with difficult conversations.

  • Week 1: Introduction to communication skills
  • Week 2: Effective listening and nonverbal communication
  • Week 3: Conflict resolution and assertiveness training
  • Week 4: Module wrap-up and participant survey

    • Module 2: Motivations   Staff (Presenter)   February 2023

  • Week 1: Understanding motivation and goal setting
  • Week 2: Identifying personal values and motivators
  • Week 3: Strategies for staying motivated
  • Week 4: Module wrap-up and participant survey

    • Module 3: Self-love   Terri Thomas (Presenter)   March 2023

    In this module we will be learning all about ourselves! We will discuss how to set personal goals, how to focus on yourself (every now and then), and get to know yourself better. We will discuss how to give yourself emotional support when making decisions alone.

  • Week 1: The importance of self-acceptance and self-compassion
  • Week 2: Building self-esteem and confidence
  • Week 3: Practicing self-care and stress management techniques
  • Week 4: Module wrap-up and participant survey

    • Parent Activity: Self-love massage

    Quarter 2

    Module 4: Co-parenting   Dr. Perry (Presenter)   April 2023

    In this module we will discuss how to develop positive communicating relationships between parenting partners including developing how to parent on a consistent level (eye-to-eye) basis, removing negative parent talk/thought, and creating a balanced parenting schedule.

  • Week 1: Effective co-parenting strategies
  • Week 2: Communication and conflict resolution in co-parenting
  • Week 3: Raising children in a blended family
  • Week 4: Module wrap-up and participant survey

    • Module 5: Self-sufficiency   Sameko Bailey (Presenter)   May 2023

    In this module we will discuss how to establish positive routines to help families become consistent. We will develop plans through brainstorming what type of schedule is desired (chores, school activities, family activities, etc.) and tips on how to follow through with the proposed plans in our everyday lives.

  • Week 1: Financial literacy and budgeting
  • Week 2: Basic home maintenance and repair skills
  • Week 3: Time management and organizational skills
  • Week 4: Module wrap-up and participant survey

    • Module 6: Accountability   Anita Moore/ Keith Jones (Presenter)   June 2023

    In this module we will discuss how to incorporate routines and foster independence through accountability. We will explore our knowledge of actions versus consequences and see if we are creating safe and healthy environments for our children/ youth. We will discuss how to involve children in deciding and establishing expected responsibilities and enforcing rules to hold them accountable.

  • Week 1: The importance of personal accountability
  • Week 2: Setting goals and tracking progress
  • Week 3: Developing self-discipline and overcoming procrastination
  • Week 4: Module wrap-up and participant survey

    • Parent Activity - TBD

    Quarter 3

    Module 7: Advocacy   Brandie C (Presenter)   July 2023

    In this module we will discuss how to be present (listen & hear) for your child(ren) and how to advocate (speak/stand up) for yourself and your child(ren). We will explain why it is important to be active and involved in parent/ teacher communication. We will discuss how to celebrate your child(ren)’s accomplishments.

  • Week 1: Understanding advocacy and its role in social change
  • Week 2: Identifying personal advocacy goals and strategies
  • Week 3: Effective communication and public speaking skills
  • Week 4: Module wrap-up and participant survey

    • Module 8: Conflict Resolution   Staff (Presenter)   August 2023

    * Week 1: Recognizing and understanding conflict styles * Week 2: Developing conflict resolution strategies * Week 3: Mediation and negotiation techniques

  • Week 4: Module wrap-up and participant survey

    • Module 9: Retraining Our Brains   Ms. Summers (Presenter)   September 2023

    In this module we will discuss how to promote and encourage honesty and positive behaviors in children and our family. We will explore the belief/ tradition of Generational curses and how to rectify the impact of them.

  • Week 1: The power of positive thinking and self-talk
  • Week 2: Identifying and challenging negative thought patterns
  • Week 3: Developing mindfulness and relaxation techniques
  • Week 4: Module wrap-up and participant survey

    • Parent Activity - Baxter Avenue Theater

    Quarter 4

    Module 10: Power   Dr. Hollowell (Presenter)   October 2023

    In this module we will discuss how to create power through cultivating positivity and value in doing the right things. We will explore what power we have in our lives and how to remove/ minimize things (negative people, places, things, etc.) that intend to take away our power. We will also discuss the power in monitoring cell phones and social media exposure.

  • Week 1: Understanding the concept of power and its impact on relationships
  • Week 2: Identifying personal sources of power and influence
  • Week 3: Empowerment strategies for personal and professional growth
  • Week 4: Module wrap-up and participant survey

    • Module 11: Coping   Derrick Mitchell (Presenter)   November 2023

    In this module we will discuss how to support your family’s coping skills through promoting healthy social skills and friendships. We will discuss how physical activity and spiritual/ religious beliefs provide positive effects in coping with life’s many troubles and concerns

  • Week 1: Healthy coping mechanisms for stress and difficult emotions
  • Week 2: Identifying and understanding personal triggers
  • Week 3: Developing resilience and stress management techniques
  • Week 4: Module wrap-up and participant survey

    • Module 12: Mental Health/Trauma   Dawn Hale/ Sandra Marshall King (Presenter)   December 2023

    In this module we will discuss how mental health and trauma are connected and how to encourage seeking help without feeling inadequate. We will discuss how abuse (physical/ emotional/ sexual/ substance), stress, loss, and violence can cause an overwhelming state of mind and how to positively address these issues.

    *Warning- This module may bring up negative memories and feelings when discussing things such as grief, abandonment, rejection and anger. Please let staff know if you need help to find resources in your local area to deal with these feelings.*

  • Week 1: Understanding mental health and trauma
  • Week 2: Recognizing signs of mental health challenges
  • Week 3: Seeking help and support for mental health concerns
  • Week 4: Module wrap-up and participant survey
